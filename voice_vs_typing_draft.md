# voice_vs_typing — Draft synthesis

Synthesis of the speed/quality/QoL thought-experiment thread (messages 3886-3914 in `Telegram_calls.md`) into the argumentative arc for the writing sample. Eight movements:

1. The question: is the speed gain real? — distinguishing speed from QoL.
2. The freedom ladder L0-L4, calibrated in days assuming knowledge and arc are already in hand.
3. The fatigue-decay typing model — speed `s(n)` decays, error-correction overhead `ε(n)` grows, per-message time `t(n) = 1000/s(n) + ε(n)`, terminate at `t(m) > 60 min`. Result: 19 messages, 12.77 hours.
4. Daily cap with overnight reset: 6-hour session, 11 messages/day, 11,000 words/day steady-state.
5. Reading time as irreducible shared overhead: 10 min per assistant reply. 2-day typing run becomes 916 min wall clock. Voice equivalent: 337 min. Compression: 2.72×.
6. Quality decay and the two-axis Pareto picture: quality drops 2% per message for typing, constant for voice. With inter-day fatigue, 3-day stretched session, 30 messages, typing avg quality 69% vs voice 100%. Quality-adjusted throughput compression: 4.11×.
7. The painful-input vs pleasant-input regime — the deepest framing. Voice doesn't merely compress time; it changes WHAT THE HUMAN OWNS in the collaboration. Painful-input pushes human to QA-only role; pleasant-input restores human's authorial role.
